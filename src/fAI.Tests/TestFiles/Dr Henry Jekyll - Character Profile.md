# Dr. Henry Jekyll - Character Profile

Full Name: Dr. Henry Jekyll, M.D., D.C.L., LL.D., F.R.S.
Occupation: Physician, Surgeon & Research Scientist
Setting: Victorian London, late 19th century, England

## Background
Dr. Jekyll is a wealthy, highly educated, and widely respected member of London's upper-class scientific community. Described as a tall, well-made man in his fifties, he is known for his warmth, charm, and philanthropic nature. He holds an impressive string of academic degrees, signaling mastery across multiple disciplines.

## Medical & Scientific Specialties

Biochemistry / Pharmacology — His primary obsession. Jekyll dedicates years to researching chemical compounds and their effects on the human body, ultimately synthesizing the transformative potion central to the story.
Psychology & Human Nature — Jekyll is deeply fascinated by the duality of the human mind — the coexistence of good and evil within a single person. Much of his theoretical work bridges medicine and philosophy.
General Medicine & Surgery — As a practicing Victorian doctor, he holds expertise in clinical medicine, with colleagues such as Dr. Lanyon representing the established medical mainstream he gradually diverges from.
Experimental Science — Jekyll pushes well beyond conventional medicine into uncharted territory, conducting private experiments he knows the scientific community would reject or condemn.


## Fatal Flaw
Despite his brilliance, Jekyll's work is driven by a deeply personal motive — a desire to liberate himself from Victorian moral constraints — rather than purely scientific curiosity. This blurring of personal ambition and science is ultimately what leads to his destruction.


"I have been made to learn that the doom and burthen of our life is bound forever on man's shoulders, and when the attempt is made to cast it off, it but returns upon us with more unfamiliar and more awful pressure."
— Dr. Henry Jekyll
